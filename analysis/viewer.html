What this tool is trying to answer

One simple question: is there a better day and hour to post on Civitai? If you're about to upload an image, does it matter whether you do it on a Tuesday afternoon or a Saturday night — or is it all the same?

To answer that honestly, the tool looks at thousands of the most-reacted images on Civitai, checks when each one was posted, and compares that against when images get posted in general. The gap between those two is the answer.

Where the data comes from

A companion script (analyze-posting-times.js) talks to Civitai's public API and gathers two separate piles of information. It does not download any pictures — for each image it only notes a couple of plain facts.

Pile 1 — "the winners" (the Top pull)

It asks Civitai for its most-reacted images, working from the all-time champions downward, across every content rating. For each one it records when it was posted and how many reactions it got (likes + hearts + laughs + cries). Everything else is thrown away. These winners are sorted into calendar-year buckets, keeping ~5,000 per year.

Pile 2 — "everybody" (the Baseline pull)

Then it grabs a big sample of recent uploads sorted by newest, ignoring popularity — the famous and the ignored mixed together. This answers "when do people normally post?"

The key idea: "lift"

If 40% of all uploads happen Saturday evening, then ~40% of winners will be too — but that's just crowd timing, not advantage. So instead the tool computes lift:

lift = (winners' share of a day/hour) ÷ (everybody's share of that day/hour)

  • lift > 1 (red) → over-performs: a genuinely good time to post.
  • lift < 1 (blue) → under-performs.
  • lift ≈ 1 → no advantage.

Reading the charts & exploring

  • Faded heatmap cells have few images behind them, so their lift is statistically noisy — trust the solid cells. Use the Confidence selector up top to fade more or less aggressively.
  • Click any bar in the "per month" timeline (or the per-year month chart) to drill into that month — its own day×hour heatmap and reaction distribution appear.
  • Reaction spread by year shows the middle-50% range and median, so you see how spread out each year's reactions were, not just the average.
  • Timezone shifts the time-based charts to your local hours (data is stored in UTC).

Honesty notes

  • Month/timeline charts are raw counts, not lift — the baseline only covers recent weeks, so it can't fairly judge "best month" of years past.
  • Older images had longer to collect reactions, which affects cross-year reaction sizes, not the within-year timing pattern.
  • We only look at winners — lift controls for volume but can't say why a slot wins.
  • Before you load real data, the numbers are synthetic demo data (with a planted "Tue–Thu afternoon wins" pattern) just to show the layout.

Refreshing the data

Re-run the pull where it can reach Civitai (your PC or a GitHub Action): node analyze-posting-times.js. That writes posting-analysis.json (loaded here) and posting-raw.json (slim raw data). Future view changes recompute instantly with node analyze-posting-times.js --in posting-raw.json — no second API trip. Then load the JSON here via Choose File.
